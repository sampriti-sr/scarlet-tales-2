I know the most valuable aspect of your life is your academic achievements. You want to make a mark wherever you go but you are troubled when other people (who have scored less than you) succeed.
As your partner, I can only tell you that we have our unique trajectories in life and comparison will kill us from inside so here is a story for you which I think will help you go through this year.

Once there was a man who felt very sad because his hard work never seemed to give results. One day, he went to the forest and met a wise hermit.
The man asked, “Why should I not give up?” The hermit showed him two plants — a fern and a Chinese bamboo.
He said, “I planted both at the same time and gave them water and care every day. The fern grew quickly, but the bamboo did not grow at all for many years.”
The man was surprised.
Then the hermit smiled and said, “In the fifth year, the bamboo suddenly grew very tall — almost as tall as a building!”
The man asked, “Why did it take so long?” The hermit replied, “Because the bamboo was growing strong roots under the ground first.”
The man understood the lesson — sometimes we grow on the inside before others can see it. He felt happy and went back to live his life with hope.
Just because you don’t see results quickly doesn’t mean you’re not growing. Keep trying — your roots are getting stronger.

Happiest Birthday.
